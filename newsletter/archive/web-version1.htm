a list of arby's thoughts
rewatched TRUE STORIES the other day which is one of my favorite movies
i could say many things but "it is epic" and i have wild wild life stuck in my head
recently i read LIVE CINEMA and ITS TECHNIQUES by francis ford coppola
which i found to be a very funny book but extremely thought provoking in terms of constructing visual language for live film that differentiates itself between stuff like theater, classic live television, and traditional cinema. i think the book is only a few years old but he is accurately predicting cable two in my opinion.
the book also fueled my obsession with GORDON CRAIG'S large modular walls, his vision for theatre design revolved around the use of blank, flexible screens that could be moved and arranged for different scenes. these screens, consisting of hinged and stationary flats, were used to emotionally affect audiences rather than "natural representations"

here's his conceptual art and the final result of his innovative production of HAMLET for constantin stanislavsky at the moscow art theatre in 1912.
